About

What is Relief Call?
Relief Call is a nonprofit venture started by a bunch of engineers to help Chronic Kidney Disease patients. Our concentration is mainly on unprivileged patients who are under HaemoDialysis and we’re restricted to Mysore region.

How it started?
Everything begins with passion, with feelings and a story behind it. Every new beginning comes from some other beginning’s end. Relief Call is such a beginning that hit our head when a trauma called dialysis ended in one of our friend’s family.
When we realized that there are so many out there who really can’t afford a prescribed number of dialysis per week due to financial problems, a toddler called ‘Relief Call’ came into existence to help those who are in need of financial help for HaemoDialysis treatment.

Why we started Relief Call?
Kidney failure or CKD is one such disease where the treatment is really not affordable. We can break down the cost of treatment as follows,

  1. Dialysis Cost.
  2. Dialyser Cost.
  3. Regular Medicines.
  4. Blood work every month.
  5. Regular Checkups.
  6. Blood transfusions.
  7. Emergency can hit anytime.

Physical trauma the patient goes through and the emotional trauma the family faces is unbearable added to it one has to be financially stable as the treatment is costly and not affordable to all.

One has to ideally get dialysed thrice a week which counts up to 12 dialysis per month. Relief Call’s aim is to collect funds from the people who are willing to donate for this cause and help few unprivileged patients by providing a financial help through subsidized dialysis after a thorough background check done by our volunteers. Join our hands and support us for the cause. After all, one human can save another.

Sigh of Relief

“At first his legs got swollen, he doesn’t feel hungry now and he’s deteriorating day by day. Dialysis is prescribed for three days a week. The treatment is very costly and all we can afford is two dialysis a week.” Said the wife of a CKD patient.

This is when we, the bunch of engineers got this idea of “Relief.” Relief is a budding venture to help Chronic kidney disease patients right now at the stage of survey. Radhika Subramanya

I still remember the day when I heard the term dialysis for the first time. I was in 5th grade and it was my dad who said that his dear sister is undergoing dialysis. I could feel the love, care, affection and fear in my dad’s voice when he stated that people undergoing dialysis might not live much longer. And a week after, we received the sad news of death of my aunt who breathed her last at her third dialysis procedure. Thats when I realized how deadly a disease chronic kidney disorder is! Well, there were many such incidents which got me closer to dialysis patients! One of my mother’s colleague, my mother’s brother, a relative of a dearest friend and the list goes on! This made me realize that CKD is one of the most commom diseases nowadays! Infact, one of the facts states that “Every year, kidney disease kills more people than breast or prostate cancer”. There are many difficulties a dialysis patient goes through. The main reason is the treatment being not really affordable! 2-3 dialysis per week, expensive regular medicines/injections/surgeries, travel cost and what not! As per one of the analysis, dialysis expenditures for patients over a long period rank the highest among all major traumas and diseases. And in India, majority of patients on dialysis are ‘self paying’ because of limited health insurance coverage available from government as well as private insurance providers. Being not able to bear the expenses, having a feeling of being burden to their family, going through the dialysis pain- all these might affect a patient’s mental health and force him to enter depression. One of the studies state that dialysis patients diagnosed with depression are nearly twice as likely to be hospitalized or die within a year as those who are not depressed. When we think of the ways to help a dialysis patient, there is nothing much we can do! We cant share their physical pain. All we can do is to find some ways to keep patients mentally healthy and strong and make them realize that they are not alone in their journey. And the only way that we can achieve this is by helping under previliged financially. Sacrificing a small part of our expense will turn into a great relief in CKD patient’s life. Please join our hands in making CKD patient’s life better and bearable! After all, every human being on earth has the right to be happy! #reliefcall
